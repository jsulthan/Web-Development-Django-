While it might seem like extra work at a first glance, here are some key moments in which prototyping will come in handy:


  1. 1. Usability testing. Does your user know how to exit out of screens? Can they follow your intended user journey and buy something from the site you’ve designed? By running a usability test, you’ll be able to see how users will interact with your design once it’s live;

  2. 2. Involving stakeholders. Need to check if your GDPR consent boxes are displaying properly? Pass your prototype to your data protection team and they can test it for real;

  3. 3. Impressing a client. Prototypes can help explain or even sell your idea by providing your client with a hands-on experience;

  4. 4. Communicating your vision. By using an interactive medium to preview and test design elements, designers and developers can understand each other — and the project — better.
